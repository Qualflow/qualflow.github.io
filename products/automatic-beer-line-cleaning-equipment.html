VerxClean: Fixed True Automatic Beer-Line Cleaner for Pub Companies & Large Venue Operators

Derived form the Latin word “Verax” meaning true or truthful, Qualflow’s VerxClean ™ is the first beer-line cleaner to deliver true automatic beer-line cleaning. VerxClean is designed for pub companies, restaurant chains and large venues to ensure the line cleaning process is standardized across multiple outlets.

True and proper automation is essential to standardizing the cleaning process and is key to excellent beer quality, which in turn drives beer sales, keg yields, and group profitability.

Optional Bluetooth and Verify app provides real-time monitoring of beer-line cleaning visits and clean quality data.







Patent pending Smart Hose pressure balanced technology distributes detergent and water flow to all lines even in pubs with split lines and the keg store located on any floor. Keeping the line pressurised during the entire line clean ensures full detergent contact and best in class line hygiene.

PLCS: Portable Beer-Line Cleaning Systems for Dispense Technicians & Quality Service Teams

Designed with brewers and quality service teams, the PLCS™ is the most cost-effective way to distribute the benefits of beer-line cleaning equipment across multiple customer outlets.

PLCS - Automatic with Vortex

Vortex uses the mechanical energy of turbulence to enhance the chemical action of detergents and rinse water to lift and scrub biofilm and yeast from the beer-line.

Compared to soak cleaning, Vortex improves line cleaning efficacy by up-to 50% allowing faster cleaning time and extended cleaning cycles.

The systems high capacity allows up to 14 line cleans per cycle.



PLCS - Automatic

The PLCS Automatic makes the process of cleaning beer-lines economic, faster, better and safer compared to manual line cleaning methods. Housed in a military specification case, the PLCS is designed to clean beer-lines on an industrial scale.




PLCS - Manual

The PLCS Manual is designed to deliver turbulent flowing detergent and rinse water. Turbulent flowing detergent is up to eight times more effective at cleaning beer-lines compared to wash bottles.
